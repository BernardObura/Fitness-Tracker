Barbell Bench Press

Chest, Shoulders, Triceps Intermediate

A compound exercise that targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps by pressing weight upward while lying on a bench. The bench press is one of the three exercises in the sport of powerlifting and is used extensively in weight training, bodybuilding, and other types of training to develop the chest muscles.

Tips and Common Mistakes
Tips
  • Keep your feet flat on the floor for stability.
  • Maintain a slight arch in your lower back.
  • Keep your shoulders retracted and pressed into the bench.
  • Lower the bar in a controlled manner to mid-chest.
  • Keep your wrists straight and knuckles pointing toward the ceiling.
Common Mistakes
  • Bouncing the bar off your chest.
  • Lifting your hips off the bench.
  • Not keeping your wrists straight.
  • Flaring your elbows out too far.
  • Using too much weight and sacrificing form.

Step-by-Step Instructions
1
Setup

Lie on a flat bench with your feet flat on the floor. Your eyes should be directly under the barbell. Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, with your palms facing forward.

2
Unrack the Bar

Lift the bar off the rack by straightening your arms. Move the bar directly over your shoulders with your arms fully extended.

3
Lower the Bar

Inhale and slowly lower the bar to your mid-chest (around the nipple line) while keeping your elbows at about a 45-degree angle to your body. Keep your shoulders back and down into the bench.

4
Press the Bar

Exhale and push the bar back up to the starting position by extending your arms, making sure to keep your wrists straight and not letting them bend back. The bar should move in a straight line up.

5
Repeat

Perform the desired number of repetitions, maintaining proper form throughout the movement.

6
Rack the Bar

After completing your set, carefully rack the bar back onto the supports. Don't try to rack the bar by looking up, instead feel for the rack as you push the bar back.
